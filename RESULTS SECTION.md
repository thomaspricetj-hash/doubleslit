\# Results  

\### Numerical Behavior of the Information‑Compression Model



The simulation reproduces key quantum phenomena and reveals quantitative

relationships between information state and observable patterns.



\## 1. Visibility vs Compression

Sweeping c from 0 to 1 produces a smooth reduction in interference visibility.

The numerical data closely follows a linear trend:



&#x20;   V(c) ≈ 1 − c



This provides a clear, testable prediction for partial which‑path experiments.



\## 2. Collapse and Packet Formation

Detector-triggered compression produces a localized Gaussian packet whose width

matches the kernel applied during collapse. Changing detector geometry modifies

the packet shape, confirming the model’s prediction that collapse is

geometry-dependent.



\## 3. Re‑Decompression and Interference Recovery

Removing which‑path information restores interference. The degree of recovery

matches the degree of decompression, demonstrating a continuous transition

between particle-like and wave-like behavior.



\## 4. Hysteresis

Different compression histories leading to the same final c produce measurably

different visibility values. This indicates that information-state transitions

can exhibit hysteresis, a feature absent from standard interpretations.



\## 5. Timing Effects

Delaying re‑decompression reduces the maximum recoverable visibility. This

suggests that the duration of compression influences the coherence that can be

restored, providing another experimentally testable prediction.
