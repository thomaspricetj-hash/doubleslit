Quantum mechanics describes physical systems using wavefunctions that evolve smoothly until measurement, at which point they appear to collapse discontinuously.

The Information‑Compression Model proposes a different interpretation:



Collapse is not destruction.

Collapse is reversible compression.



Wave‑like behavior corresponds to decompressed information.

Particle‑like behavior corresponds to compressed information.

Measurement forces compression.

Erasure forces decompression.



This framework provides a unified, information‑theoretic description of quantum behavior.
