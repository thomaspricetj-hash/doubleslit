\## 1. Interference Visibility as a Function of Compression Strength



The model introduces a continuous information‑state variable `c ∈ \[0,1]`:



\- `c = 0` → fully decompressed (pure wave)

\- `c = 1` → fully compressed (pure particle)



\*\*Prediction:\*\*  

Interference visibility `V` decreases smoothly and predictably with compression.

The simulation suggests a near‑linear relationship:





V(c) ≈ 1 − c





This can be tested experimentally using:



\- variable‑strength which‑path detectors  

\- weak measurements  

\- tunable polarizers or beam splitters

\## 2. Information Hysteresis (History‑Dependent Behavior)



Standard quantum mechanics treats coherence loss as effectively irreversible once

information is recorded.



The Information‑Compression Model predicts \*\*hysteresis\*\*:



compress → decompress → compress





may produce a different final interference pattern than:



compress (once) to the same final level





even if the final information state is identical.



This can be tested by:



\- writing which‑path information  

\- erasing it  

\- re‑writing it  

\- comparing final fringe visibility

\## 3. Timing Sensitivity of Re‑Decompression



In delayed‑choice quantum eraser experiments, the timing of erasure does not

affect the final interference pattern.



The model predicts a subtle effect:



\*\*Prediction:\*\*  

If information remains compressed for a long duration before decompression,

the maximum recoverable interference visibility may decrease slightly.



This can be tested by:



\- delaying the erasure event  

\- measuring fringe visibility as a function of delay time
