VARIABLE‑STRENGTH WHICH‑PATH INTERFEROMETER



Purpose:

Test the predicted linear relationship between compression (c) and visibility (V).



Setup:

\- Mach‑Zehnder interferometer

\- Variable‑strength which‑path detector (polarizer, phase shifter, or weak measurement device)

\- Single‑photon source

\- High‑resolution detectors



Procedure:

1\. Prepare photons in a 50/50 superposition.

2\. Gradually increase which‑path detector strength from 0 to 1.

3\. Measure interference visibility at each step.



ICM prediction:

V = 1 - c

(near‑linear decline)



Standard QM prediction:

Visibility declines non‑linearly based on coherence loss.



Falsifiability:

If visibility does not follow a monotonic, near‑linear curve, ICM is falsified.



HYSTERESIS TEST: COMPRESS -> ERASE -> RE‑COMPRESS



Purpose:

Setup:

\- Double‑slit or Mach‑Zehnder

\- Weak which‑path detector

\- Eraser (polarizer or quantum eraser module)

\- Re‑application of which‑path marking



Procedure:

compress -> erase -> compress

Sequence B:

Measure final interference visibility for both sequences.



ICM prediction:

A and B produce different visibility.



Standard QM prediction:

A and B produce identical visibility.



Falsifiability:
