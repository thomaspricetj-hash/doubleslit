\# Theory  

\### Information‑Compression Interpretation of Wave–Particle Behavior



This model treats quantum behavior as transitions between information states.

Instead of assuming that “wave” and “particle” are separate ontological modes,

the model defines them as two ends of a continuous information spectrum.



A quantum entity is represented by a wavefunction ψ(x, y) whose spatial extent

and coherence depend on an information‑state parameter:



&#x20;   c ∈ \[0, 1]



where:

\- c = 0 → fully decompressed information (wave-like)

\- c = 1 → fully compressed information (particle-like)



Measurement is modeled as a forced compression event. When a detector acquires

which‑path information, the wavefunction is multiplied by a localized kernel,

producing a directional Gaussian packet. Removing information (as in quantum

eraser setups) corresponds to re‑decompression, allowing the wavefunction to

expand and recover interference.



This framework provides:

\- a mechanistic explanation of collapse,

\- a continuous transition between wave and particle behavior,

\- a natural interpretation of partial which‑path information,

\- and a unified description of interference, decoherence, and re‑coherence.



The model is implemented numerically in this repository and produces

experimentally testable predictions.
